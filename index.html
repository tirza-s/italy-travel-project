Why Dolomites ?

Experience the Magic of the Dolomites

Nestled in the heart of the Alps, the Dolomites are a UNESCO World Heritage site offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and outdoor thrills. Whether you’re a skier, hiker, or simply seeking relaxation, this stunning mountain range has something for everyone.

Scenic Hiking Trails for Every Skill Explore trails that offer stunning views – from easy walks to challenging hikes up majestic peaks. Summer is the perfect time to experience alpine meadows and crystal-clear lakes, allowing you to connect with nature in the most beautiful setting.

Charming Villages Rich Culture, Discover picturesque towns like Cortina d’Ampezzo, where Italian and Tyrolean traditions come together. Explore the charming streets, enjoy local hospitality, and savor alpine dishes and wines that define the region's rich cultural heritage.

Year-Round Natural Beauty The Dolomites offer something incredible no matter when you visit. In winter, the region’s snow-covered peaks are breathtaking. In summer, enjoy lush valleys and pristine lakes. With its diverse landscapes, the Dolomites are a perfect year-round getaway.

Top Things to Do in the Dolomites

Must-Try Adventures & Experiences

Hiking at Col Raiser

Col Raiser is one of the best places to hike in the Dolomites. A quick cable car ride takes you straight into breathtaking alpine scenery, so you get incredible views without a tough climb.

Address:

Str. Raiser, 65, 39048 Santa Cristina Valgardena BZ, Italy

What I like about it:

The trails are easy to follow, with options for both casual walkers and serious hikers. Along the way, you’ll pass through lush meadows, rugged peaks, and peaceful mountain huts where you can stop for a break.

Ski at Cortina d'Ampezzo

Skiing in Cortina d'Ampezzo is truly unforgettable. The resort offers pristine slopes, breathtaking mountain views, and a wide range of runs for all abilities.

Address:

P.tta San Francesco, 8 - 32043 Cortina d'Ampezzo, Italy

What I like about it:

It's a perfect balance of great skiing and the charming town atmosphere. After a day on the slopes, you can unwind in cozy cafés or enjoy the vibrant après-ski scene. Cortina’s it a must-visit destination for any ski enthusiast.

Learn more

Biking at South Tyrol

Biking in the Dolomites, especially in South Tyrol, is an amazing experience. Every year, the most difficult mountain bike race in the Dolomites attracts a multitude of cyclists.

Address:

Südtirol Dolomiti Superbike Rienzstraße 31/ via Rienza 31, I-39039 Villabassa/Niederdorf

What I like about it:

Beautiful mountains and charming villages and peaceful surroundings make it even better. Whether you’re just enjoying a relaxed ride or testing your skills on tougher routes, South Tyrol is a great place for biking.
